Listening to any members only Audio version of any story in Medium

This story is going to be about how I was able to listen to the audio versions of popular stories, which, otherwise, are for premium members only — not accessible for free without becoming a member.

From Medium FAQ :

What do I get as a member?
When you upgrade, you’ll unlock all the stories published for members by top writers and leading experts on Medium. (When you read and clap for those stories as a member, part of your $5/month goes directly to that author.) You’ll also be able to listen to audio versions of hundreds of popular stories stories handpicked by our editors. And you’ll be able to save stories to your offline reading list and archive.

Basically, writers can select if they want to publish their stories publicly or for subscribed members only. And any non-subscriber can read up to 3 subscriber stories per month for free.

Medium also has audio versions of these stories accessible to subscribed members only. Non-subscribers can listen to just 3 of these per month.

Let’s look at this example. This series has around 6 parts and all of them are subscribed members only and audio version of the story is available.

After reading 2 stories this is what you will see. As you can see, you can play the audio version of the story.

If you open the 4th story. Since, we exhausted the 3 story limit the audio version is not available.

And only the first paragraph is visible and you can’t read the entire story.

Since this is a new feature I got curious and started digging around and found out that if you know the URL of the post. Most likely you would because Medium encourages authors to share the work in social media.

The link looks like this

https://medium.com/s/personal-geography/the-city-burning-9cec234df901

The last 9cec234df901 is the ID for this story. You can do a GET request to https://medium.com/p/ID/notes (https://medium.com/p/9cec234df901/notes)

I have highlighted the audioVersionUrl parameter in the response.

Now you can open it in any browser and play it.

TLDR:

If you have the ID of the premium member only post you can just take that ID and go to https://medium.com/p/ID/notes and get the audioVersionUrl parameter value and open it in new tab in your browser and enjoy the audio version of the Premium members only story.

I reported this to Medium and they replied saying:

Our engineers have concluded that your report does not meet the criteria outlined in the Bug Bounty program (https://medium.com/policy/mediums-bug-bounty-disclosure-program-34b1c80764c2), and therefore is not eligible for a reward.

Since they do not consider it a security vulnerability, I can blog about it. If you are interested, you can go ahead and listen to some audio good stories in Medium.
